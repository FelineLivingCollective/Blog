### 2. The 15-Minute Sweet Spot

Let's be honest, who has time for three-hour tabletop sagas?

Hidden Fish is my secret weapon for a sophisticated, low-friction gathering. It lasts **10 to 15 minutes** maximum, is easy to explain, and always generates high-value laughs. It is **quick fun** for a midweek gathering, or the perfect ice-breaker when waiting for dinner guests to arrive.

The core loop is simple: Every player receives a set of **Treat tokens** (values 1, 2, or 3) and secretly chooses how many cards they want to bet on. This element of **perfect for guests** fun is what I call high-value entertainment — big laughs in a small timeframe.

### 3. The Best Kind of Bet: The Push Your Luck Thrill

The mechanics are addictive. Hidden Fish is primarily a **Push Your Luck** game built around **imperfect information**.

The central deck is composed of 30 Fish (Victory Points) and 10 "Bone" cards (Penalties). The tension of peaking at your secret Treat Token and betting high is **viciously thrilling**. My heart accelerates every time I flip the second or third card from the central stack, knowing that hitting two or more Bones means a painful BUST and losing the round.

It's the perfect balance between simple strategy (card counting matters as Bones are permanently discarded) and pure luck, which means it's genuinely **family fun** that appeals to everyone at the table.
